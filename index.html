Board games history

Board games were with people for thousands of years. The oldest known playable board game is the Royal Game of Ur. Its origin is dated around 4.600 BC. According to British Museum in this game two players compete to race their pieces from one end of the board to the other.

Another old game is Senet (see pic.). It was found in Predynastic and First Dynasty burials of Egypt, c. 3500 BC and 3100 BC respectively.

Hounds and jackals, another ancient Egyptian board game, appeared around 2000 BC.

The oldest records of board gaming in Europe date back to Homer's Iliad (written in the 8th century BC), in which he mentions the Ancient Greek game of Petteia.

Many traditional game are still popular in China (Go and Chess), in Africa and Middle East (Mancala) or India (Carrom).


Modern Times

In 20th Century board games have become more and more popular. From around year 2000, board game industry started rapidly growing. In 2015 over 5000 new games and expansions have been released. That's over 13 new games every day!

Some of board game enthusiasts have collection reaching hundrends or ever thousands of games!

The biggest website and online database is BoardGameGeek. It was created in 2000 by two friends and now it stores information about 140.000 games.


Board Game Conventions

Board games fans can attend to many events and conventions dedicated to board games. There they can try new games before they are released, meet favourite designers, compete in tournaments, meet other gamers with similar interests, hear talks and panels about topics in the industry and obviously play a lot of games.

The biggest board game convention is Internationale Spieltage SPIEL in Essen Germany. It's an annual four-day board game fair. It began in 1983. In 2019 over 209.000 people visited SPIEL.
